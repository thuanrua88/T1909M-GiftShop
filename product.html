This puzzle depicts sweets and confectionery from throughout the 1970s. Made up of 500 carefully illustrated pieces of high quality, 100% recycled puzzle board.At the beginning of the 1970s three pence could buy a huge range of treats. However, as the decade went on, inflation pushed up prices and a single chocolate bar could cost up to 6p! Sweet shops were expanding their ranges with new and exciting brands including Double Decker, Nutty, Chunky Yorkie, Old Jamaica, Buttersnap and Curly Wurly. There were also experimental variations like a Caramac with minty fudge or a dipped flake. The jigsaw is part of the Robert Opie collection from Gibson Games and is presented in an fabulous gift tin.
